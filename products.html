Glycols

MEG (Monoethylene Glycol)

A colourless, odourless, and viscous organic compound (HOCH₂CH₂OH) — the simplest form of ethylene glycol, primarily used across a wide range of industrial applications.

Monoethylene glycol (MEG) is a colorless, odorless, and viscous organic compound with the chemical formula HOCH₂CH₂OH. It belongs to the family of glycols and is the simplest form of ethylene glycol (EG), which has two hydroxyl groups attached to adjacent carbon atoms. MEG is primarily used in industrial applications including antifreeze formulations, polyester production, and as a chemical intermediate.
  • High-purity industrial grade
  • Used in antifreeze & coolants
  • Polyester & resin production
  • Available in bulk volumes

Glycols

DEG (Diethylene Glycol)

A colourless, odourless, hygroscopic organic compound — [(HOCH₂CH₂)₂O] — a member of the glycol family with an additional ethylene glycol unit for extended-chain applications.

Diethylene glycol (DEG) is a colorless, odorless, and hygroscopic organic compound with the chemical formula (HOCH₂CH₂)₂O. It is a member of the glycol family, similar to monoethylene glycol (MEG) but with an additional ethylene glycol unit. DEG is widely used as a solvent, plasticiser, and in the manufacture of resins, as well as in personal care and textile processing.
  • Hygroscopic solvent properties
  • Used in resins & plasticisers
  • Textile & personal care applications
  • Supplied in bulk

Surfactants

NPE's (Nonylphenol Ethoxylates)

A group of nonionic surfactants widely used in industrial and commercial applications, valued for their detergent and emulsifying properties across many formulations.

Nonylphenol ethoxylates (NPEs) are a group of nonionic surfactants that are widely used in various industrial and commercial applications. They are produced by ethoxylation of nonylphenol, which involves reacting nonylphenol with ethylene oxide to form a mixture of compounds with varying degrees of ethoxylation. NPEs are valued for their detergent and emulsifying properties, making them useful in cleaning formulations, textile processing, and agrochemical applications.
  • Nonionic surfactant group
  • Excellent detergent & emulsifying properties
  • Used in industrial cleaning formulations
  • Multiple ethoxylation grades available

NP9 (Nonylphenol Ethoxylate-9)

A specific nonionic surfactant produced by ethoxylation of nonylphenol with nine moles of ethylene oxide, commonly used in industrial and commercial applications.

NP9 refers to Nonylphenol Ethoxylate-9, which is a specific type of nonionic surfactant belonging to the family of nonylphenol ethoxylates (NPEs). NP9 is produced by ethoxylation of nonylphenol with nine moles of ethylene oxide, resulting in a mixture of compounds with different degrees of ethoxylation. NP9 is commonly used in various industrial and commercial applications due to its detergent and emulsifying properties, similar to other NPEs, including use in textile processing, cleaning products, and agrochemicals.

Antifreeze

Antifreeze

A fluid or chemical compound added to cooling water systems to lower freezing point and raise boiling point, protecting automotive engines, HVAC systems, and industrial equipment.

Antifreeze refers to a fluid or chemical compound added to water in a cooling system to lower its freezing point and raise its boiling point. It is primarily used in automotive engines, HVAC systems, and industrial equipment to prevent the coolant from freezing in cold temperatures and from boiling in hot conditions. Common antifreeze formulations are based on ethylene glycol or propylene glycol, often combined with corrosion inhibitors and other additives.
  • Lowers freezing point of coolants
  • Raises boiling point for hot climates
  • Suitable for automotive & HVAC systems
  • Industrial equipment protection
